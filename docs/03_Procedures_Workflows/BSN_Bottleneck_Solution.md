# Breaking the BSN Vicious Cycle

The BSN registration catch-22 is the most significant hurdle for international students in the Netherlands. The cycle typically looks like this:
**No BSN → No Dutch Bank Account → No Rental Contract → No Address to Register for BSN.**

ReloNL implements a three-pronged strategy to break this loop for students.

## 🛠️ The Actionable Solutions

| Strategy | Description | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RNI Registration** | Registering at the *Registratie Niet-Ingezetenen* (Non-Residents Records) before arriving or immediately upon arrival. This provides a BSN valid for 4 months without a permanent address. | Students arriving early or those with short-term initial housing. |
| **University Days** | Leveraging dedicated registration days organized by universities (e.g., TU/e, UvA) where municipalities set up pop-up desks for students. | Students with confirmed university admission in major cities. |
| **BSN-Friendly Housing** | Prioritizing temporary housing providers (like The Social Hub or specific student hotels) that explicitly allow BRP registration for short-term stays. | Students without a permanent contract but needing a BSN immediately. |

## 🔗 Partner Integration: Phone & IBAN
To further simplify the process, ReloNL integrates with **MGMCO** via the partner link `https://mgmco.nl/jagohu`. This allows students to:
1. Set up a **Dutch Phone Line** pre-arrival.
2. Get guided support for **IBAN Setup** (using digital banks like Revolut or Trade Republic) which often don't require a BSN immediately, unlike traditional Dutch banks.
