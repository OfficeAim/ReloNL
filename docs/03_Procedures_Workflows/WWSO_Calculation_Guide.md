# WWSO Calculation Procedure

The **Woningwaarderingsstelsel (WWS)**, or the point system, is critical for both students and landlords under the 2025 Dutch housing regulations (*Wet betaalbare huur*). ReloNL provides a "Legal Shield" by automating this calculation.

## 📊 Core Calculation Inputs

The calculator processes several dimensions to determine the legal rent limit:

| Category | Factors Considered |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Physical Space** | Surface area (m²), outdoor space, and parking availability. |
| **Quality** | Energy label (A++++ to G), kitchen quality, and bathroom quality. |
| **Valuation** | WOZ value (€) and neighborhood type. |
| **Age** | Construction year (relevant for specific point multipliers). |

## ⚖️ Legal Status Classification

Based on the total points, the property is classified into one of three zones:

1. **🟢 Green (Compliant):** The current rent is below or equal to the legal maximum.
2. **🟡 Yellow (Borderline):** The rent is very close to the limit; a municipal check is recommended.
3. **🔴 Red (Over Limit):** The rent exceeds legal limits. Mistakes here can cost landlords up to **€100,000 in fines**.

## 📄 Output & Export

ReloNL generates a comprehensive **WWSO Radar Chart** visualizing the point distribution. Users can export this as a **PDF Report**, which is ready for submission to municipalities or as part of a rental contract.

> **Legal Note:** Since January 2025, landlords are legally required to provide a WWSO score with every new contract. ReloNL makes this compliance free and instant.
